E-Filing Income Tax Returns Online in India - An Overview

In India, filing an income tax return is mandatory for individuals and entities that meet certain criteria. Here are the reasons why you need to file an income tax return in India:

1. Legal requirement: Under the Income Tax Act, 1961, individuals and entities meeting specified income thresholds are required to file an income tax return. Failing to do so can result in penalties and legal consequences.

2. Income calculation and disclosure: Filing an income tax return allows you to calculate your total income for the financial year accurately. It includes income from various sources such as salary, business/profession, capital gains, rental income, and other taxable sources. By filing a return, you disclose your income to the tax authorities.

3. Determining tax liability: Income tax returns enable individuals and entities to determine their tax liability. Through the return, you report your income, claim deductions, exemptions, and avail tax benefits, if eligible. It helps in calculating the final tax payable or the refund owed by the tax department.

4. Claiming deductions and exemptions: By filing an income tax return, you can claim deductions and exemptions available under the Income Tax Act. These include deductions for investments such as provident fund contributions, life insurance premiums, health insurance premiums, home loan interest, and other eligible expenses. Availing these deductions can reduce your taxable income and, consequently, your tax liability.

5. Carry forward of losses: Filing an income tax return is necessary to carry forward and set off losses incurred during the financial year. Losses from business/profession, capital gains, or other sources can be offset against future profits, reducing tax liability in subsequent years. However, this benefit is available only if you file a tax return within the due date.

6. Compliance and documentation: Filing income tax returns ensures compliance with tax laws and demonstrates your adherence to legal requirements. It provides documentary evidence of your income, deductions, and tax payments, which can be useful for various financial and legal purposes. It establishes your financial history and can serve as proof of income for loans, visa applications, and other transactions.

7. Avoiding penalties and scrutiny: Filing an income tax return within the prescribed due date helps avoid penalties imposed by the tax department. Non-filing or delayed filing can attract penalties, interest, and scrutiny from tax authorities, leading to potential legal consequences and additional financial burden.

It's important to consult a tax professional or refer to the current Income Tax Act and rules to understand the specific requirements and obligations regarding filing income tax returns in India.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Last Date for Filing Income Tax Returns ?

  • My company deducts TDS, how should I get refund of TDS?

    If your company deducts TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) from your salary or other payments, you can claim a refund of the excess TDS amount when filing your income tax return.

  • Should I also file a return of income when I'm at a loss?

    Yes, it is generally advisable to file a return of income even if you have incurred a loss during the financial year. By filing a return of income, you can carry forward the loss incurred during the financial year for set-off against future income. This can help reduce your tax liability in subsequent years when you have profitable income. However, to carry forward the losses, it is essential to file the tax return within the specified due date.

  • Should I attach any documents along with the ITR forms?

    Technically an income tax return form does not require attachment of any proof or documents. However, you have to provide these documents in proper order in case of any inquiry.

  • When I have to mandatorily file ITR?

    In case of Individual/HUF If your total income exceeds the basic exemption limit (currently ₹2.5 lakh for individuals below 60 years of age), you are required to file an income tax return. For Companies, including private limited companies, public limited companies, and LLPs (Limited Liability Partnerships), are required to file income tax returns irrespective of the profit or loss incurred.

  • What is the due date of Filing of ITR?

    In India, the due date for filing income tax returns depends on various factors such as the category of taxpayer, the type of income, and the income amount. Here are some general guidelines for filing income tax returns in India:

    Individuals and HUFs (Hindu Undivided Families):
    The due date for individuals and HUFs is usually July 31st of the assessment year (e.g., for the financial year 2022-23, the due date would be July 31, 2023). However, the government may announce extensions or specific due dates for certain categories of taxpayers.
    Companies and firms:
    The due date for filing income tax returns for companies is generally September 30th of the assessment year.
    Tax Audit and Transfer Pricing:
    a. If you are a taxpayer who is required to undergo a tax audit under the Income Tax Act, the due date for filing the income tax return is generally extended to October 31st of the assessment year.
    b. Taxpayers involved in certain international transactions are required to file their tax returns by November 30th of the assessment year.

    It's important to note that the due dates mentioned above are subject to change, and it's advisable to refer to the official announcements and notifications from the Income Tax Department or consult a tax professional for the most accurate and up-to-date information.
    Additionally, it's crucial to maintain proper documentation, comply with tax laws, and file the income tax return within the specified due date to avoid penalties, interest, and other consequences associated with late or non-filing of returns.
